**6. Float (Slack)** ğŸ”¥ğŸ”¥ğŸ”¥

**Definition:** Time activity can be delayed without delaying project

**Formulas:**

**Key Points:**

- Non-critical activities = Positive float
- More float = More scheduling flexibility

**7. Schedule Compression** ğŸ”¥ğŸ”¥ğŸ”¥

**A. Fast Tracking:**
- Do activities in PARALLEL (were sequential)
- Advantages: Saves time, no extra cost
- Disadvantages: INCREASES RISK, may cause rework

**B. Crashing:**
- Add MORE RESOURCES to activities
- Advantages: Saves time, lower risk
- Disadvantages: INCREASES COST, diminishing returns

**When to Use:**
- **Fast Track:** Limited budget, activities can overlap, accept higher risk
- **Crash:** Have budget, need lower risk, resources available

**8. Resource Optimization** ğŸ”¥ğŸ”¥

**A. Resource Leveling:**
- Based on resource AVAILABILITY
- Adjusts dates when resources available
- **SHIFTS critical path**
- May EXTEND project

**B. Resource Smoothing:**
- Uses FLOAT to optimize resources
- Redeploys from non-critical to critical
- **Does NOT shift critical path**
- May SHORTEN project

| Aspect | Leveling | Smoothing |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| Based On | Availability | Float |
| Critical Path | Shifts | No change |
| Duration | May extend | May shorten |
